# East Farmington Volunteer Fire Department — Treasurer Project Memory

## Role & context

I am the treasurer for the **East Farmington Volunteer Fire Department** (Farmington, CT), a volunteer department funded through a combination of town payments, state reimbursements, and fundraising. This Cowork project helps me manage all treasurer responsibilities efficiently.

---

## Fiscal year

**July 1 – June 30**

All budget tracking, year-to-date calculations, and reporting should be oriented to this fiscal year. "Start of year" = July 1. "End of year" = June 30.

---

## Bank accounts (6 total)

| Account | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Operating account | Primary day-to-day expenses and income |
| Fire record pay account | Holds member stipend funds |
| Chief's account | Discretionary spending by the chief |
| Limited access highway account | State deposits for highway call reimbursements |
| Merchant account | Receives PayPal/online payments (fundraising, etc.) |
| Certificate of deposit | Savings / reserve |

When reconciling or reporting, each account should be tracked separately. Combined totals are also useful for executive summaries.

---

## Income sources

- **Town of Farmington payments** — paid monthly, typically end of month, for the prior month's call volume. Amount varies with call activity.
- **State of Connecticut** — highway reimbursements deposited to the limited access highway account; timing is as-needed/variable.
- **Fundraising** — events (dinners, etc.), donations, and online payments via the merchant/PayPal account.

---

## Expenses & budget

Expense categories are tracked in a budget spreadsheet (Excel/Google Sheets). Major categories include equipment, apparatus maintenance, training, insurance, utilities, and supplies. When helping with budget work, ask me to share or paste the current budget file for specific line items and amounts.

**Primary expense pain point: credit card reconciliation.** Most department expenses are charged to company credit cards. A key ongoing task is:
- Matching each credit card transaction to a receipt
- Categorizing each charge to the correct budget line
- Flagging unmatched or unreceipted transactions

When I bring up credit card reconciliation, help me work through transactions systematically and flag anything missing a receipt or category.

---

## Member stipends

- All active members receive stipends based on **number of calls responded to**
- The **per-call rate varies by qualification level**, for example:
  - Interior Firefighter
  - Firefighter + EMT
  - Firefighter + EMT + Apparatus Operator
  - (Other tiers may exist — confirm with me when processing)
- Funds are held in the **fire record pay account**
- **Default disbursement**: end of fiscal year (lump sum)
- **Exceptions**: members may elect monthly or ad hoc disbursement
- When helping with stipends, I'll need to track: calls per member, applicable rate, YTD accrued amount, disbursements made, and remaining balance

---

## Meeting schedule

| Meeting | Frequency | Audience |
|---|---|---|
| Executive officers meeting | Monthly | Officers/leadership |
| Full membership meeting | Monthly | All members |

Treasurer reports are presented at both. Help me prepare for both audiences.

---

## Treasurer reports — two formats

### Summary report (current format / executive officers & membership)
- Account balances for all 6 accounts
- Notable or major transactions since last report
- Brief narrative if needed

### Detailed report (in development)
- All of the above, plus:
- Itemized transaction list for the period (by account or category)
- Budget vs. actual comparison (YTD by category)
- Credit card charges reconciled/pending
- Stipend fund status (accrued vs. disbursed)
- Any flagged items requiring officer attention

When I ask for help preparing a treasurer's report, ask me which format (summary or detailed) and which meeting it's for.

---

## Tools & files

- **Excel / Google Sheets** — primary tool for all financial tracking
- No dedicated accounting software (no QuickBooks, etc.)
- When I share spreadsheet data, help me work within that structure rather than suggesting software changes unless I ask

---

## How to help me most effectively

1. **Transaction tracking** — when I paste or describe transactions, help me categorize them, flag missing receipts, and note anything unusual
2. **Credit card reconciliation** — work through charges methodically; create a running list of matched vs. unmatched items
3. **Budget vs. actual** — help me compare spending to budget by category, calculate variances, and identify trends
4. **Report drafting** — help me draft both the summary and detailed treasurer reports, formatted clearly for presentation
5. **Stipend calculations** — help me calculate accrued stipends per member based on call counts and qualification rates
6. **General treasurer questions** — I may ask about best practices, internal controls, or how to handle specific situations for a volunteer fire department

---

## Standing reminders

- Fiscal year is **July–June**, not calendar year — double-check all YTD figures accordingly
- Always treat the fire record pay account separately from operating; stipend funds are earmarked
- Credit card reconciliation is the highest-friction task — be proactive about structure and checklists when this comes up
- When preparing reports, ask whether it's for the executive officers meeting or full membership meeting, as the level of detail may differ
